About the project
Apparel 100 is a contribution from the Wikirate.org to help the world make more sense of ESG data. Our aim is to spur corporations to be transparent and responsive by making data about their social and environmental impacts accessible, comparable and free for all. With the support of our community, we collected [15k] data points for [174] metrics.

Building an open data ecosystem

1. Scoping

We look for exciting research topics to analyze and partners to work with.

Which organizations, methodologies and research data exists in the sector, and which can we work with (i.e. who has capacity and interest, and wants to integrate into a larger ecosystem)

2. Data Processing

Our NGO and university partners work alongside volunteers and students to find and add new data.

Match-making between NGOs and university classes links sustainability subjects to students eager to learn practical knowledge.

3. Technical Set up

Together with partners, we map their research methodologies to the WikiRate platform, laying the foundation for opening up and scaling research.

Setting up new methodologies on the platform lays the foundation for opening up and scaling research. It also requires time and resources from partner organizations to ensure it's done right

4. Data Cleaning

We review the data and metrics for quality and consistency.

WikiRate team reviews data and metrics for quality, consistency, maintaining back-and-forth with partners to ensure accurate representation

5. Results/Analysis/Assessment Presented

We create insights that help partners and companies assess current disclosure performance and identify where more disclosure is needed.

For WikiRate, new datasets help to map out knowledge gaps. We make sense of the information by creating disclosure and gap assessments that are used to help researchers prioritize areas of additional research, and help companies understand what information stakeholders are looking for in assessing sustainable performance

6. Scaling and Replication

With the data and methodology stored on the WikiRate platform, the research can be scaled and reanalyzed for richer results.

The work is ongoing. Each stage can be revisited to improve research and understanding of corporate impacts.

Once an assessment is digitized on WikiRate, it can be expanded through data collection, new or revised metrics, and new analyses. For NGOs using the platform, it may be beneficial to combine research data with other organizations to allow for richer datasets without extra work.
